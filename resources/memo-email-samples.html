Genre Sample: Internal Memos & Emails

Internal Memos & Emails

Three examples of institutional communications. Notice the audience, tone, and level of detail in each.

To: All Faculty
From: Dr. Patricia Chen, Interim Provost
Date: September 3, 2025
Subject: Fall 2025 Syllabus Policy Updates

Dear Colleagues,

As we begin the fall semester, I am writing to share several updates to university syllabus policy that take effect immediately. These changes were developed in consultation with the Faculty Senate and reflect feedback from both faculty and students over the past year.

Key changes include:

  • All syllabi must now include a statement on the use of generative AI tools, specifying what is and is not permitted in the course. A template statement is available on the Provost's website.
  • Attendance policies must specify whether participation is factored into the final grade and, if so, how it is calculated.
  • The required accessibility statement has been updated to reflect current federal guidance. Please use the revised language posted on the Academic Affairs site.

Updated syllabi should be uploaded to the course management system by September 15. If you have questions about any of these changes, please contact your department chair or the Office of Academic Affairs.

Thank you for your continued dedication to our students.

Patricia Chen, Ph.D.
Interim Provost and Vice President for Academic Affairs

To: English Department Faculty and Staff
From: Dr. James Owusu, Dean, College of Arts & Sciences
Date: March 12, 2025
Subject: Restructuring of First-Year Writing Program

Dear Colleagues,

I am writing to inform you that, effective Fall 2026, the College will be restructuring the First-Year Writing Program. After reviewing enrollment data, student outcomes, and feedback from the program review conducted last spring, we have decided to consolidate ENGL 101 and ENGL 102 into a single two-semester sequence with a shared curriculum.

This change is intended to improve consistency across sections and better support students who arrive with varying levels of preparation. The new sequence will include standardized learning outcomes while preserving instructor flexibility in selecting readings and assignments.

A working group will be formed this semester to develop the new curriculum. If you are interested in serving on this committee, please contact Dr. Maria Santos in the Writing Program by March 28.

I recognize that changes like this can raise questions. I will hold two open forums for discussion:

  • Wednesday, March 26 at 3:00 p.m. in Harmon Hall 204
  • Thursday, April 3 at 11:00 a.m. via Zoom (link to follow)

James Owusu, Ph.D.
Dean, College of Arts & Sciences

To: All Currently Enrolled Students
From: Office of the Registrar
Date: November 8, 2025
Subject: Spring 2026 Registration Opens November 18

Dear Students,

Registration for Spring 2026 classes opens on Monday, November 18. Your individual registration time can be found in your student portal under "Registration Appointments."

Before you register, please make sure you have:

  1. Met with your academic advisor (a hold may be placed on your account if you have not completed advising)
  2. Resolved any outstanding financial holds
  3. Reviewed the Spring 2026 course schedule, now available online

If you experience any issues during registration, contact the Registrar's Office at registrar@university.edu or visit our office in Administration Building, Room 110, Monday–Friday from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.

Office of the Registrar
Lakewood State University
