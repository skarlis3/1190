How to Save a Web Page as a PDF

This page covers three common scenarios you'll run into when you need to save something as a PDF for class. Pick whichever method applies to your situation.

Method 1: Print to PDF (Any Web Page)

This is the simplest method and works on any web page. It saves exactly what you see on screen — ads, sidebars, and all. If you're saving a news article and want a cleaner result, skip down to Method 2.

Method 2: News Articles & Social Media (Reader Mode + Print to PDF)

Why Reader Mode?

News sites and social media pages are full of ads, pop-ups, and clutter. Reader Mode strips all of that away so your PDF is clean and readable — just the article text and images.

I do not recommend using Google Chrome for this. Chrome's reading mode is a side reader and doesn't always show content in full. Use Microsoft Edge, Mozilla Firefox, or Safari instead.

File Naming Tip

Always name your files clearly (e.g., "NYT-Housing-Article.pdf" or "Smith-2023-Rhetoric.pdf") so you can find them later. Save them somewhere consistent, like a class folder in your Documents or on your cloud drive.
