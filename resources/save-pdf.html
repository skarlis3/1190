How to Save a Web Page as a PDF

This page covers three common scenarios you'll run into when you need to save something as a PDF for class. Jump to whichever one applies to your situation.

On This Page

  • Method 1: Print to PDF (Any Web Page)
  • Method 2: News Articles & Social Media (Reader Mode + Print to PDF)
  • Method 3: Scholarly / Journal Articles

Method 1: Print to PDF (Any Web Page)

This is the simplest method and works on any web page. It saves exactly what you see on screen — ads, sidebars, and all. If you're saving a news article and want a cleaner result, skip down to Method 2.

PC (Windows)

  1. Open the page you want to save in any browser.
  2. Press Ctrl + P to open the print dialog.
  3. In the Printer dropdown, select "Microsoft Print to PDF".
  4. Click Print, then choose where to save the file and give it a clear name.

Mac

  1. Open the page you want to save in any browser.
  2. Press ⌘ Cmd + P to open the print dialog.
  3. In the bottom-left corner, click the PDF dropdown button and choose "Save as PDF".
  4. Name your file and click Save.

iPad

  1. Open the page in Safari (or your preferred browser).
  2. Tap the Share button (the square with an arrow pointing up).
  3. Scroll down and tap "Print".
  4. On the print preview, pinch outward (zoom in) on the page preview with two fingers. This converts it into a PDF preview.
  5. Tap the Share button again and choose "Save to Files".

Key Step for iPad

The pinch-to-zoom trick is the important part — it converts the print preview into a PDF you can save.

Android Tablet

  1. Open the page in your browser.
  2. Tap the three-dot menu (⋯) and choose "Share" or "Print".
  3. If you chose Share, select "Print" from the share options.
  4. At the top of the print screen, tap the printer dropdown and select "Save as PDF".
  5. Tap the Save button and choose where to save it.

Method 2: News Articles & Social Media (Reader Mode + Print to PDF)

Why Reader Mode?

News sites and social media pages are full of ads, pop-ups, and clutter. Reader Mode strips all of that away so your PDF is clean and readable — just the article text and images.

Do not use Google Chrome for this. Chrome does not have a built-in Reader Mode. Use Microsoft Edge, Mozilla Firefox, or Safari instead.

On a Computer (PC or Mac)

Pick whichever browser you have installed. The steps are very similar across all three.

Microsoft Edge (PC or Mac)

Step 1 — Turn on Reader Mode

  1. Open the article in Edge.
  2. Look at the address bar — you should see a small book icon on the right side. Click it.
  3. The page will reload in a clean, simplified view. That's Reader Mode!

Don't see the book icon?

Not every page supports Reader Mode. Try pressing F9 on PC, or look under the three-dot menu (⋯) → Immersive Reader.

Step 2 — Print to PDF

  1. Press Ctrl + P on PC, or ⌘ Cmd + P on Mac.
  2. In the Printer dropdown, choose "Microsoft Print to PDF" (PC) or "Save as PDF" (Mac).
  3. Click Print (or Save on Mac).
  4. Choose where to save the file, give it a clear name, and click Save.

Mozilla Firefox (PC or Mac)

Step 1 — Turn on Reader Mode

  1. Open the article in Firefox.
  2. Look at the address bar for a small page/document icon on the right side. Click it.
  3. The page switches to a clean reading view.

Keyboard Shortcut

You can also press F9 to toggle Reader Mode on and off in Firefox.

Step 2 — Print to PDF

  1. Press Ctrl + P on PC, or ⌘ Cmd + P on Mac.
  2. In the Destination or Printer dropdown, choose "Microsoft Print to PDF" (PC) or "Save as PDF" (Mac).
  3. Click Print / Save.
  4. Name your file and save it somewhere you can find it (like your Desktop or Downloads folder).

Safari (Mac Only)

Step 1 — Turn on Reader Mode

  1. Open the article in Safari.
  2. In the address bar, click the page icon on the left side (next to the URL). Select "Show Reader".
  3. The page will switch to a clean view.

Step 2 — Print to PDF

  1. Press ⌘ Cmd + P.
  2. In the bottom-left corner of the print dialog, click the PDF dropdown button and choose "Save as PDF".
  3. Name your file and click Save.

On a Tablet

iPad (Safari)

Step 1 — Turn on Reader Mode

  1. Open the article in Safari.
  2. Tap the Aa button in the address bar (or the page icon, depending on your iOS version).
  3. Tap "Show Reader" to get the clean view.

Step 2 — Save as PDF

  1. Tap the Share button (the square with an arrow pointing up).
  2. Scroll down and tap "Print".
  3. On the print preview, pinch outward (zoom in) on the page preview with two fingers. This turns it into a PDF preview.
  4. Now tap the Share button again and choose "Save to Files".

Android Tablet (Firefox or Edge)

Step 1 — Turn on Reader Mode

  1. Open the article in Firefox or Edge.
  2. Firefox: Look for the Reader Mode icon in the address bar and tap it.
  3. Edge: Tap the three-dot menu (⋯) and look for "Immersive Reader".

Step 2 — Print to PDF

  1. Tap the three-dot menu and choose "Share" or "Print".
  2. If you chose Share, select "Print" from the share options.
  3. At the top of the print screen, tap the printer dropdown and select "Save as PDF".
  4. Tap the Save button and choose where to save it.

Method 3: Scholarly / Journal Articles

When you find an article through our library databases (like EBSCO, JSTOR, or ProQuest), you'll usually land in one of two situations. Here's how to handle each one.

If the Article Opens on the Journal's Website

Many databases link you out to the journal's own site, where the article appears as a regular web page with text, images, and navigation around it.

  1. Look for a "Download PDF" or "View PDF" button or icon — it's usually near the top of the article, in a toolbar, or in a sidebar. It may also appear as a small PDF icon.
  2. Click it. The article will either download directly or open as a PDF in your browser.
  3. If it opens as a PDF in your browser, see the next section to save it.

Can't Find a Download Button?

Some journal sites bury the PDF link or only show it after you scroll past the abstract. Look for icons that look like a downward arrow, a document page, or the letters "PDF." If you still can't find it, try the basic Print to PDF method as a fallback.

If the Article Opens as a PDF Document

Sometimes the database or journal site opens the article directly as a PDF in your browser window. You'll know this is the case because the page will look like a document with its own formatting — not like a regular web page.

On a Computer (PC or Mac)

  1. Hover your mouse near the top of the browser window — a toolbar should appear with icons for zoom, print, and save.
  2. Click the download/save icon (usually a downward arrow or a floppy disk icon).
  3. Choose where to save the file, give it a clear name, and click Save.

Toolbar Not Appearing?

Try right-clicking anywhere on the PDF and selecting "Save As" or "Download PDF." You can also press Ctrl + S (PC) or ⌘ Cmd + S (Mac).

On an iPad

  1. When the PDF opens in Safari, tap the Share button (the square with an arrow pointing up).
  2. Choose "Save to Files" and pick your preferred location.

On an Android Tablet

  1. When the PDF opens in your browser, tap the download icon (downward arrow) in the toolbar at the top of the screen.
  2. The file will save to your Downloads folder. You can move it from there if needed.

File Naming Tip

Always name your files clearly (e.g., "NYT-Housing-Article.pdf" or "Smith-2023-Rhetoric.pdf") so you can find them later. Save them somewhere consistent, like a class folder in your Documents or on your cloud drive.
