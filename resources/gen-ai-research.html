Specialized AI Research Tools

You may use specialized AI research tools to help locate academic sources. These tools are designed to search scholarly databases and can help you find relevant articles more efficiently.

  • Semantic Scholar — AI-powered academic search engine
  • Research Rabbit — finds related papers based on articles you provide
  • Scite — shows how articles have been cited (supported, contrasted, or mentioned)
  • Consensus — AI search engine focused on research findings
  • Connected Papers — visual tool that maps related research
  • Elicit — AI research assistant for finding and summarizing papers

Important Guidelines

  • Read the original articles. Do not rely on AI-generated summaries. You must read and engage with the actual source material.
  • Cite the original sources. All quotes and paraphrases must come from the original articles, not from AI-generated text.
  • Fact-check AI-provided information. AI tools can "hallucinate" or produce incorrect or incomplete data, including fabricating sources that don't exist.
  • Be cautious with general-purpose AI tools. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and similar chatbots are not designed for academic research accuracy and may misrepresent information or invent sources. Use the specialized tools above instead.
