How to Save an Email as a PDF

Some assignments ask you to submit a PDF of an email (for example, a confirmation email from the Academic Success Center). Here's how to do it on different devices.

On a Computer (PC or Mac)

  1. Open the email you need to save.
  2. Open the print dialog:
    • PC: Press Ctrl + P
    • Mac: Press ⌘ Cmd + P
    • You can also look for a print icon within your email app or click File > Print.
  3. Change the printer/destination to "Save as PDF" (Mac) or "Microsoft Print to PDF" (PC).
  4. Click Print or Save.
  5. Name your file clearly (e.g., "Tutoring-Visit-1.pdf") and save it somewhere you can find it later.

Using Gmail or Outlook in a Browser?

The same steps work — open the email, press Ctrl + P (PC) or ⌘ Cmd + P (Mac), and change the printer to PDF. In Gmail, you can also click the three-dot menu inside the email and select "Print".

On an iPad

  1. Open the email in the Mail app (or Gmail, Outlook, etc.).
  2. Tap the Share button (the square with an arrow pointing up). In some apps, you may need to tap the three-dot menu (…) first, then look for "Print".
  3. Tap "Print".
  4. On the print preview, pinch outward (zoom in) on the preview with two fingers. This converts it into a PDF.
  5. Tap the Share button again and choose "Save to Files".

On an Android Tablet or Phone

  1. Open the email in your email app.
  2. Tap the three-dot menu (⋯) and select "Print".
  3. At the top of the print screen, tap the printer dropdown and select "Save as PDF".
  4. Tap the Save button (usually a download icon) and choose where to save the file.

Make Sure You Save the Right Email

If you're saving a tutoring confirmation, make sure the email shows the consultation notes from your visit — not just the appointment confirmation. The consultation notes email is the one that comes after your visit is complete.
