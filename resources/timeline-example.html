Example Timeline

AI in Higher Ed & Writing Studies (Dystopian / System Failure)

I wrote this example on a large sheet of paper based on my own field (teaching college English). Here's what it looked like:

A more readable version of the same timeline is below. Notice how it branches, how some branches lead to dead ends, and how the ideas escalate as they move forward in time. Your timeline will look different — this is just to show you the process.

Current Situation

  • AI having major impact on higher ed / writing studies
  • Students overusing AI — may be impacting cognitive skills / weakening connections — ways to use going forward?
  • Faculty needing to figure out what it means for their field
Three things are happening at once
Branch 1

Literacy rates & critical thinking skills continue to decline

Branch 2

Students continue to use AI in a way where learning isn't happening

Branch 3

Online classes being overrun by agentic AI (financial aid fraud rises)

Branches 1 & 2 merge

Degrees Become Meaningless

  • Employers start giving exams or more tasks for hiring
  • On-job training programs increase (but without multidisciplinary education)

Corporate Training Takes Over

  • Training programs that are corporate run become the norm
  • People join them and then must work at that company after (and/or during)

Universities Start to Close Down

  • Liberal arts, ethics, philosophy, fine arts programs disappear
  • Focus is on being employable & productive & making company profitable
  • Civic participation lowers — fewer universities to get degrees in law (that isn't sponsored by a company)

End Result: Corporate State

  • Corporations hold all political power & employees must commit to a company in order to receive training
  • Social mobility is limited
  • Education has become an entirely corporate sponsored system
  • Ideas not supported by corporations are banned
Branch 3 continues

HLC Responds

HLC determines that online classes must require validation the students are real humans

Monitoring Increases

Monitoring increases, required in-person exams

Online Classes Disappear

  • Online classes disappear over time
  • Classes are monitored with in-person exams
  • More careers start requiring accreditation and exams
This branch isn't really going anywhere dystopian enough — just ending up with more in-person classes and testing. Going to follow the other branch instead.
