Project 2: Artifact Planning

For each event on your timeline, figure out what's happening, what kind of text would exist there, and plan the details.

1. What's happening?

Look at one event on your timeline. What's going on? What would a museum visitor need to understand?

2. What would exist here?

If this really happened, what documents, posts, or texts would people be creating? Think about:

  • Who's involved and what they'd write or post
  • What genre fits this moment?
  • Write down several ideas, then pick the strongest

3. Break it down

For the artifact you choose, plan out:

  • Year — When is this from?
  • Genre — What kind of text is it?
  • Audience — Who reads/sees this?
  • Purpose — Why does it exist?
  • Content — What's the message?

Repeat for each artifact on your timeline.

Example

Timeline event:

Degrees Become Meaningless

  • Employers give exams instead of requiring degrees
  • On-job training programs increase

Chosen artifact:

Reddit Post

Year:
2035
Genre:
Reddit post
Audience:
Redditors discussing employment
Purpose:
Vent frustration, seek advice
Content:
Frustrated about required classes & exams after starting a job; worried about getting fired if they fail
