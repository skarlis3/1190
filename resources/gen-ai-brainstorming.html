Brainstorming

Idea Generation

If you use GenAI for any sort of idea generation types of brainstorming (and I suggest caution here), have it make you large lists of ideas that you can choose from.

If you use GenAI to create a lot of ideas, it can help get some ideas going while still allowing you to think through the suggestions and make the best choices.

Whereas, if you have GenAI generate just one idea and stick with it, that idea might:

  1. Not work well for the assignment, and
  2. Not be an idea that you feel like you can write about.

If you don't understand the idea enough to write about it, you'll be more likely to want AI to help you with the next step and the next.

Thinking Partner

When set up / prompted thoughtfully, GenAI can work as a thinking partner for brainstorming. The idea is to use it to help push your ideas instead of to come up with the ideas for you. If you choose to use AI in this way, I strongly suggest spending some time working out a prompt or "prompt add-on" that will keep it from attempting to just think for you.

Some suggestions:

I need help working out my thoughts on a project. Do not automatically agree with me. Ask questions, push back when appropriate and help me externally process my ideas. Do not try to create anything for me.

Then include what you are trying to do, such as a summary of the assignment, what you've done so far, and where you are stuck.

You may need to refine the above prompt to work best for you and the GenAI tool you are using. Also try different tools to see what works better for this kind of work. My experience is that some AI tools just really want to give you the answer (often the incorrect or oversimplified answer, at that), whereas other AI tools can step back and just support your thinking better. While the first type may seem more helpful, it really isn't. You are the one in the class and doing the work, and GenAI is typically going to lean towards more generic responses that aren't always the most interesting, recent, or relevant to our specific class.

An Example

When brainstorming a new assignment, I've tried using different AIs to help me work through what I was trying to do and what the best way to get to that objective might be.

Google Gemini (in the fall of 2025, at least) continued to push me towards assignments that focused on the "end product" (instead of space for learning and failure) and really wanted me to grade on grammar. This is likely because its training data found far more assignments and lessons for writing classes that had those focuses. Therefore, it's weighted to give those responses (i.e. it is always going to push towards the "most likely"). Even telling it to not do that had really limited impact.

I eventually moved over to using Claude (Opus 4.5 at the time), and it did much better with my instructions. I eventually created a teaching philosophy document that I could just copy and paste into a chat whenever I wanted to do brainstorming work so that I could actually work through the ideas without the AI pushing me in a particular direction, particularly not a direction I would never take when teaching.

Once it let me work through ideas myself, instead of trying to push me one way or the other, I found that I was able to use its input to deepen my thinking and was able to develop better assignments for it. In the past, it would have taken a few semesters of trial and error to get to the same place I was able to get to after a brainstorming session.

As one note, I still think a human thinking partner of someone else in my field would be even more helpful. That said, we can all only do so much of this labor for each other as teachers (listening to others' ideas and helping them brainstorm). I don't have someone available to me seven days a week, so I'm finding that GenAI can help fill that gap.
