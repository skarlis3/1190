Generative AI Policy

If you choose to use Generative AI in this class, you are responsible for understanding and following these guidelines. Be aware that AI policies differ by instructor and class—always review each course's expectations.

At a Glance

  • Brainstorming: AI can help you generate ideas for topics, but the conclusions must be your own.
  • Research: You may use specialized AI tools to find sources, but you must read and cite the original articles—not AI summaries.
  • Writing & Revision: Draft first, then use AI to clarify or polish small sections. Review all output carefully.
  • Voice & Accuracy: Keep your own voice and double-check all facts; AI can be incorrect or overly generic.
  • Best Use: Treat AI as a tool for organization or feedback—not as a writer or a source of ideas.

Brainstorming

You may use AI tools to help generate ideas or topics. However, the main content, writing, research, and conclusions must be your own. Use AI brainstorming results only as a starting point, not as material to include directly. Do not take or adapt AI-generated ideas as if they were research.

See GenAI Brainstorming for detailed guidance and example prompts.

Research

You may use specialized AI research tools to help locate academic sources. You must read the original articles, cite them directly, and always fact-check AI-provided information. Be cautious with general-purpose AI tools—they are not designed for academic accuracy.

See Using AI for Research for a list of recommended tools and guidelines.

Writing & Revision

AI can be useful for revising, organizing, or proofreading your own drafts—after you've already written them. Write out your own ideas first, work on small sections at a time, and review all output carefully. Protect your writing voice.

See GenAI for Revision & Proofreading for detailed approaches and example prompts.

Understanding the Issues with AI Writing

AI-generated text often appears polished but lacks depth, accuracy, and originality. It tends to overgeneralize, repeat ideas, and miss nuance—especially in academic contexts. AI is best used as a tool—for organization, feedback, and mechanical assistance—not as a writer or source of ideas.

See Using GenAI for more on this topic.

AI Transparency Statement

If you use GenAI for any assignment, include an AI Transparency Statement at the end of your submission. Your Project 1 revised draft requires a detailed statement regardless of whether you used AI.

See AI Transparency Statement for formatting guidelines and examples.
