APA Style & Citations

A quick-reference guide for citing sources in APA Style (7th edition). Click any card below to see the formula, an example, and key notes. For the full APA rules, see the additional resources at the bottom of this page.

Paper Formatting

Page Setup

Page Setup

  • Double-space the entire paper (including the References page).
  • Use a readable 11–12 pt font (e.g., Times New Roman 12 pt, Calibri 11 pt).
  • Set 1″ margins on all sides.
  • Indent the first line of each paragraph by 0.5″.
  • Include a page number in the upper-right corner of every page.
Title Page

Title Page

Your title page should include the following, centered on the upper half of the page:

  • Paper title (bold)
  • Your name
  • Course name and number
  • Instructor’s name
  • Due date
References Page

References Page Setup

  • Start on a new page.
  • Center the word References (bold) at the top.
  • List entries alphabetically by the first element (usually the author’s last name).
  • Double-space all entries.
  • Use a hanging indent: first line flush left, subsequent lines indented 0.5″.
  • Do not add a period after a DOI or URL.

In-Text Citations

Parenthetical

Parenthetical Citation

Place the author and year in parentheses at the end of the sentence, before the period.

Example
AI grading tools have raised concerns about fairness in higher education (Martinez, 2024).
Key Notes
  • Two authors: use & — (Chen & Sharma, 2024)
  • Three+ authors: first author + et al. — (Rodriguez et al., 2023)
  • No author: first few words of title in quotes — (“AI Hiring Tools,” 2024)
Narrative

Narrative Citation

Name the author in the sentence; place the year in parentheses right after the name.

Example
Martinez (2024) found that AI grading tools have raised concerns about fairness in higher education.
Key Notes
  • Two authors: use “and” (not &) — Chen and Sharma (2024)
  • Three+ authors: first author + et al. — Rodriguez et al. (2023)
Direct Quotes

Direct Quotes

When quoting exact words, add a locator (page, paragraph, or section) after the year.

Example
Martinez (2024) argued that the tools “lack the nuance of human judgment” (p. 12).
Key Notes
  • Page number: (p. 12) or (pp. 12–14)
  • Paragraph number: (para. 3)
  • No page or paragraph? Use a section heading or other descriptor to help the reader find the passage.

Reference Entries

Social Media Post

Social Media Post

Formula
Author or Username. (Year, Month Day). First 20 words of the post [Format]. Platform. URL
Example
u/dataworklife. (2024, October 15). Does anyone else feel like AI is being pushed into every part of our workflow without anyone asking if [Online forum post]. Reddit. https://www.reddit.com/r/datascience/comments/example
Key Notes
  • If the author’s real name is known: Last Name, F. I. [@username].
  • Describe the format in brackets: [Post], [Video], [Image attached], [Online forum post].
  • Reproduce post text exactly — including hashtags, emojis, and abbreviations.

In-text: (u/dataworklife, 2024)

Online News Article

Online News Article

Formula
Author Last Name, F. I. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. Name of Publication. URL
Example
Metz, C. (2024, September 12). The race to make AI-generated content detectable. The New York Times. https://www.nytimes.com/2024/09/12/technology/example
Key Notes
  • Italicize the publication name, not the article title.
  • Article title in sentence case (capitalize only the first word, the first word after a colon, and proper nouns).
  • No author? Start the entry with the article title.

In-text: (Metz, 2024)

Scholarly Journal Article

Scholarly Journal Article

Formula
Author, F. I., & Author, F. I. (Year). Title of article. Journal Title, Volume(Issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx
Example
Chen, L., & Sharma, D. (2024). Artificial intelligence in undergraduate education: A systematic review. Computers & Education, 210, 104–121. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.compedu.2024.example
Key Notes
  • Always include a DOI when available (formatted as a URL). No period after the DOI.
  • No DOI and found in a database? End after the page range — do not include the database name or URL.
  • Three+ authors in-text: first author + et al.

In-text: (Chen & Sharma, 2024)

White Paper / Report

White Paper / Technical Report

Formula
Author or Organization. (Year). Title of report (Report No. if available). Publisher (if different from author). URL
Example
McKinsey Global Institute. (2023). The economic potential of generative AI: The next productivity frontier. https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/example
Key Notes
  • If the author and publisher are the same, list the author only.
  • Include a report number if one exists.

In-text: (McKinsey Global Institute, 2023)

Government Report

Government Report

Formula
Government Agency. (Year). Title of report (Publication No. if available). URL
Example
U.S. Government Accountability Office. (2024). Artificial intelligence in health care: Agencies have begun oversight but more action is needed (GAO-24-106780). https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-24-106780
Key Notes
  • First citation: spell out the full agency name.
  • Subsequent citations: you may abbreviate — (GAO, 2024) — after establishing it.

In-text: (U.S. Government Accountability Office, 2024)

News Video

News Video (YouTube, etc.)

Formula
Author or Organization. (Year, Month Day). Title of video [Video]. Platform. URL
Example
CNBC. (2024, March 18). How AI is transforming the hiring process [Video]. YouTube. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=example
Key Notes
  • The author is the account that uploaded the video.
  • Include [Video] in brackets after the title.

In-text: (CNBC, 2024)

Position Statement

Position Statement

Formula
Organization Name. (Year). Title of position statement. URL
Example
American Nurses Association. (2024). Position statement on the ethical use of artificial intelligence in nursing practice. https://www.nursingworld.org/practice-policy/example
Key Notes
  • First citation: spell out the full organization name.
  • Subsequent citations: you may abbreviate — (ANA, 2024).

In-text: (American Nurses Association, 2024)

Webpage

Webpage

Formula
Author or Organization. (Year, Month Day). Title of page. Site Name (if different from author). URL
Example
International Society for Technology in Education. (n.d.). AI in education. https://www.iste.org/areas-of-focus/AI-in-education
Key Notes
  • Use “n.d.” (no date) if no publication date is provided.
  • Only include a retrieval date if the page content changes regularly and is not archived.

In-text: (International Society for Technology in Education, n.d.)
