APA Style & Citations

This page covers how to format your paper and cite sources in APA Style (7th edition) for ENGL 1190. It focuses on the source types you are most likely to use for the Project 1 research paper, including social media posts, online news articles, and peer-reviewed scholarly articles.

At a Glance

  • Double-space your entire paper; use a readable 11–12 pt font (e.g., Times New Roman, Calibri, Arial).
  • Set 1″ margins on all sides.
  • Include an APA-formatted title page.
  • Provide in-text citations for all quoted, paraphrased, and summarized material.
  • Include a separate References page at the end with entries for every source cited in your paper.

APA Paper Formatting

  • Double-space the entire paper (including the References page).
  • Use a clear, readable font in 11–12 pt (e.g., Times New Roman 12 pt, Calibri 11 pt, or Arial 11 pt).
  • Set 1″ margins on all sides.
  • Indent the first line of each paragraph by 0.5″ (use the Tab key).
  • Include a title page with the paper title, your name, the course name and number, the instructor's name, and the due date.
  • Include a page number in the upper-right corner of every page (including the title page).
  • Include a References page at the end of your paper. Entries should be listed alphabetically by the first element of each entry (usually the author's last name) and formatted with a hanging indent.

In-Text Citations

Every time you quote, paraphrase, or summarize information from a source, you need an in-text citation. APA uses an author-date system. There are two main patterns:

Parenthetical Citation

Place the author's last name and the year of publication in parentheses at the end of the sentence, before the period.

AI-powered grading tools have raised concerns about fairness and transparency in higher education (Martinez, 2024).

Narrative Citation

Name the author in the sentence itself and place the year in parentheses immediately after the name.

Martinez (2024) found that AI-powered grading tools have raised concerns about fairness and transparency in higher education.

Key Rules

  • One or two authors: Include all names every time you cite the source. For two authors, use an ampersand (&) in parenthetical citations and "and" in narrative citations.
  • Three or more authors: Use the first author's last name followed by "et al." every time, including the first citation.
  • No author: Use the first few words of the title in place of the author name. Use quotation marks for article titles; use italics for titles of standalone works.
  • Direct quotes: Include a page number, paragraph number, or other locator after the year: (Martinez, 2024, p. 12) or (Martinez, 2024, para. 3). If no page or paragraph number is available, you can use a section heading or other descriptor to help the reader locate the passage.
  • Social media and web sources: If the source has no page numbers (which is common), include a paragraph number or timestamp if you are quoting directly. For paraphrases, the author and year are sufficient.

References Page

Your References page is the last page of your paper. It lists every source you cited in your paper, organized alphabetically.

Formatting the References Page

  • Center the word References (bold) at the top of the page.
  • Double-space all entries.
  • Use a hanging indent for each entry: the first line is flush left, and all subsequent lines are indented 0.5″.
  • List entries alphabetically by the first element (usually the author's last name).
  • Do not add a period after a DOI or URL, as it may interfere with the link.

General APA Reference Formula

Author. (Date). Title of work. Source. URL or DOI

The specific details change depending on the source type, but this is the underlying logic for every APA reference: who wrote it, when it was published, what it's called, and where to find it.

Citing Social Media Posts

Social media posts include Reddit threads, LinkedIn posts, Facebook posts, Instagram posts, TikTok videos, and similar platforms. For your Project 1 paper, these will likely fall under your "public conversations" sources.

Reference Format

Author or Username. (Year, Month Day). First 20 words of the post [Description of format]. Platform Name. URL

Key Rules for Social Media

  • If the author's real name is known, list their name first with the username in brackets: Last Name, F. I. [@username].
  • If only a username is available, use the username in place of the author name (no brackets).
  • Use the first 20 words of the post as the title. Count hashtags, emojis, links, and @mentions as one word each. Reproduce the text exactly as written, including any abbreviations or unconventional spelling.
  • In brackets after the title, describe the format: [Post], [Video], [Image attached], [Online forum post], [Comment on an article], etc.
  • Include the platform name (e.g., Reddit, LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok) and the URL.

Examples

Reddit Post

u/dataworklife. (2024, October 15). Does anyone else feel like AI is being pushed into every part of our workflow without anyone asking if [Online forum post]. Reddit. https://www.reddit.com/r/datascience/comments/example

In-text: (u/dataworklife, 2024)

LinkedIn Post (Real Name Known)

Patel, R. [@rajpatel]. (2025, January 8). I've been testing AI writing tools for six months in my marketing team, and here's what I've learned about quality [Post]. LinkedIn. https://www.linkedin.com/posts/example

In-text: (Patel, 2025) — or — Patel (2025)

Facebook Group Post (Username Only)

NursingNerd2024. (2024, November 3). Just saw my hospital is rolling out an AI triage system next month. Has anyone else dealt with this? What [Post]. Facebook. https://www.facebook.com/groups/example/posts/123456

In-text: (NursingNerd2024, 2024)

Citing Online News Articles

This covers articles from online newspapers and news websites. APA distinguishes between newspapers (e.g., The New York Times, The Washington Post) and news websites (e.g., CNN, BBC News, Reuters), but the format is nearly identical for online versions of either.

Reference Format

Author Last Name, F. I. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. Name of Publication. URL

Key Rules for News Articles

  • Italicize the name of the newspaper or news site.
  • Use sentence case for the article title (capitalize only the first word, the first word after a colon, and proper nouns). Do not italicize the article title.
  • If there is no individual author listed, begin the entry with the article title.
  • Include the full date (year, month, and day) when available.
  • For online articles, include the URL at the end. Do not include "Retrieved from" unless the content may change over time (which is rare for published news articles).

Examples

Online Newspaper Article

Metz, C. (2024, September 12). The race to make AI-generated content detectable. The New York Times. https://www.nytimes.com/2024/09/12/technology/example

In-text: (Metz, 2024) — or — Metz (2024)

News Website Article

Duffy, C. (2025, January 22). Hospitals turn to AI for patient intake, but nurses push back. CNN. https://www.cnn.com/2025/01/22/health/example

In-text: (Duffy, 2025)

News Article with No Author

AI hiring tools face new scrutiny from federal regulators. (2024, August 5). Reuters. https://www.reuters.com/technology/example

In-text: ("AI Hiring Tools," 2024) — use the first few words of the title in quotation marks

Citing Scholarly Journal Articles

Scholarly (peer-reviewed) journal articles are the most standard APA citation type. You will need at least four of these for Project 1.

Reference Format

Author Last Name, F. I., & Author Last Name, F. I. (Year). Title of article. Title of Journal, Volume(Issue), page range. https://doi.org/xxxxx

Key Rules for Scholarly Articles

  • Italicize the journal title and the volume number. The issue number goes in parentheses directly after the volume and is not italicized.
  • Use sentence case for the article title. Use title case for the journal name (capitalize each major word).
  • Always include a DOI when one is available. Format it as a URL: https://doi.org/xxxxx. Do not put a period after the DOI.
  • If the article does not have a DOI and you found it in an academic database (like the library databases), do not include the database name or URL. End the reference after the page range.
  • If the article does not have a DOI and you found it on the open web, include the URL.
  • For three or more authors, list all authors (up to 20) in the reference entry. In the in-text citation, use the first author's last name followed by "et al."

Examples

Journal Article with DOI (Two Authors)

Chen, L., & Sharma, D. (2024). Artificial intelligence in undergraduate education: A systematic review of student and faculty perspectives. Computers & Education, 210, 104–121. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.compedu.2024.example

In-text: (Chen & Sharma, 2024) — or — Chen and Sharma (2024)

Journal Article with DOI (Three or More Authors)

Rodriguez, A. M., Kim, J., Patel, S., & O'Brien, T. (2023). The impact of AI-assisted diagnostics on clinical decision-making in emergency medicine. Journal of Medical Internet Research, 25(4), Article e44123. https://doi.org/10.2196/44123

In-text: (Rodriguez et al., 2023) — or — Rodriguez et al. (2023)

Journal Article without DOI (from a Database)

Thompson, K. R. (2023). Automation anxiety: How entry-level workers perceive AI threats to job security. Journal of Career Development, 50(2), 312–329.

In-text: (Thompson, 2023). No URL or database name needed.

Additional Source Types

Depending on your topic, you may encounter other types of sources during your research. Below are APA formats for some common extras.

White Paper or Technical Report

Author or Organization. (Year). Title of report (Report No. if available). Publisher (if different from author). URL

McKinsey Global Institute. (2023). The economic potential of generative AI: The next productivity frontier. https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/example

In-text: (McKinsey Global Institute, 2023)

Government Report

Government Agency. (Year). Title of report (Publication No. if available). URL

U.S. Government Accountability Office. (2024). Artificial intelligence in health care: Agencies have begun oversight but more action is needed (GAO-24-106780). https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-24-106780

In-text: (U.S. Government Accountability Office, 2024). For subsequent citations, you may abbreviate: (GAO, 2024) after establishing the abbreviation the first time.

News Video (e.g., YouTube Upload from a News Organization)

Author or Organization. (Year, Month Day). Title of video [Video]. Platform Name. URL

CNBC. (2024, March 18). How AI is transforming the hiring process [Video]. YouTube. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=example

In-text: (CNBC, 2024). The author is the account that uploaded the video. If the uploader is different from the creator, credit the uploader.

Position Statement from a Professional Organization

Organization Name. (Year). Title of position statement. URL

American Nurses Association. (2024). Position statement on the ethical use of artificial intelligence in nursing practice. https://www.nursingworld.org/practice-policy/example

In-text: (American Nurses Association, 2024). For subsequent citations, you may abbreviate: (ANA, 2024) after establishing the abbreviation the first time.

Webpage on an Organization's Website

Use this format for content on an organization's website that is not a report, article, or other specific document type. This includes informational pages, "About" sections, FAQ pages, and similar content.

Author or Organization. (Year, Month Day). Title of page. Site Name (if different from author). URL

International Society for Technology in Education. (n.d.). AI in education. https://www.iste.org/areas-of-focus/AI-in-education

In-text: (International Society for Technology in Education, n.d.). Use "n.d." (no date) when no publication date is provided. If the page content may change over time, include a retrieval date: Retrieved February 10, 2026, from [URL].

When to Include a Retrieval Date

Most sources do not need a retrieval date. Only include one when the source content is designed to change over time and is not archived — for example, a social media profile page, a wiki, or a website that is regularly updated without a clear publication date. Published articles, reports, and dated web pages do not need retrieval dates.
