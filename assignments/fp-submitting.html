Submitting Your Portfolio

Important Information

  • The Final Portfolio…
  • is worth 30% of your total grade,
  • is due in Canvas by the start of class on the due date,
  • will not be accepted late, and
  • must be “checked in” to ensure that I can access/view all parts of your portfolio.

Class attendance on the due date is mandatory unless you check in your portfolio with me in advance. See the information below on how to submit your portfolio.

Check the class calendar for the due date.

On the Final Portfolio due date, you will briefly meet with me in the classroom, and I will check that I can access all parts of your portfolio. This ensures that documents uploaded correctly, links or videos work (if applicable), and there aren’t any submission mistakes that could affect your grade. Since this is due at the end of the semester, everything must be submitted on time and correctly.

Before the Due Date

  1. Submit your final revised research paper to the Final Portfolio: Research Paper assignment in Canvas as a Word document (.doc or .docx).
  2. Submit your Final Reflection to the Final Portfolio: Final Reflection assignment in Canvas. See How to Submit Your Reflection below for format-specific instructions.
  3. Double-check that both have submitted correctly — open your uploaded files in Canvas to make sure they are the right versions and that everything is viewable.
  4. Submit any extra credit you have completed by the due date as well.
  5. Plan to bring your laptop or tablet and charger to class.
    • If there are any submission problems, you will have a few minutes during the class period to resolve the issue.
    • Make sure all parts of your portfolio are on the device you bring to class or backed up on a cloud drive like Google Drive or OneDrive.
  6. If you do not have a laptop or tablet, make sure all parts of your portfolio are backed up somewhere you can access on a borrowed computer.

On the Due Date

  1. Attend class and show up on time.
  2. During class, I will:
    • Check that I can access all parts of your Final Portfolio.
    • Check that I’ve received any extra credit you’ve submitted.
  3. If there is a problem with your submission, you will have a few minutes in class to resolve the issue on your own device. If you need to borrow a laptop, let me know.

Submitting Early

If you want to submit your Final Portfolio early, it still must be checked in with me. Plan to check in your portfolio during office hours or a previous class period. Portfolios cannot be checked in over email.

How to Submit Your Reflection

How you submit your Final Reflection depends on the format you chose:

PowerPoint or Google Slides

Upload your file to the Final Portfolio: Final Reflection assignment in Canvas as a .pptx file. If you used Google Slides, download it first: File > Download > Microsoft PowerPoint (.pptx).

Google Site

Submit the published link to your site in the Final Portfolio: Final Reflection assignment in Canvas. Your site must stay live through the end of the semester. Make sure you submit the correct link — expand the instructions below.

How to Publish Your Google Site and Get the Right Link

Step 1: In the Google Sites editor, click the Publish button in the top-right corner of the page.

Google Sites editor Share Publish

Step 2: Under “Who can visit your published site,” select Not listed. Do not choose “Public” or “Restricted” — “Not listed” means anyone with the link can view it, but it won’t appear in search results.

Step 3: Click Publish.

Step 4: Copy the published URL. You can find it by clicking Publish again and selecting View published site, then copying the URL from your browser’s address bar.

Make Sure You Have the Right Link

The published URL looks like:
sites.google.com/view/your-site-name

If the URL contains /edit or /d/, you are looking at the editing link, not the published site. I will not be able to view your site with the editing link. Go back and copy the URL from the published view instead.

Want to use a different format for your reflection? Check with me first to get it approved.
