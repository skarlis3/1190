Project 2: Museum Exhibit from the Year 2050

Overview

For Project 2, you are going to take the research and conclusions you drew from Project 1 to create a multimodal "museum exhibit" display. You will include artifacts from your Project 1 and new fictional artifacts from the near future, up until 2050, to tell the story of how AI changed your field — pushed to an intentionally exaggerated extreme.

The Premise

The year is 2050. You are curating an exhibit on how AI changed your field. But here's the twist: your exhibit tells the over-the-top version of the story. The best-case scenario pushed to its most utopian extreme, or the worst-case scenario pushed to its most dystopian extreme. Think big, go wild, have fun with it.

Then, tucked inside your exhibit, there's a classified document — a reality check. Based on your actual research from Project 1, what was probably going to happen? (Think of it like the alternative endings from the movie Clue: "But here's what really happened...")

  • Take research/conclusions from Project 1.
  • Create a museum exhibit for the year 2050.
  • Include artifacts from Project 1 (the past) and new fictional artifacts from the near future (2027–2050).
  • Push the story to an exaggerated extreme — utopian or dystopian.
  • Include a classified document that offers the more realistic, nuanced version.

Step 1: Decision Time

First, you need to decide what direction AI in your field went — and then take it to an extreme. You can choose from the following options:

  • The Golden Age (Utopian Extreme): The most optimistic research was correct — and then some. AI didn't just help the field; it solved everything. Push it until it's almost too good to be true.
  • System Failure (Dystopian Extreme): The warnings were ignored and the worst predictions came true — and then it got worse. Push it until it's genuinely alarming.

The point is to exaggerate on purpose. Your exhibit should escalate from where things are now (your real artifacts) to an extreme version of the future (your fictional artifacts). This is the sensationalized version of the story — your classified document is where your real, research-grounded thinking lives.

For either path, make sure your starting point is rooted in the conversations and research from Project 1. The escalation should feel like a logical (if extreme) extension of what's actually happening now.
