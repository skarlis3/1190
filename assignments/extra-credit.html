Extra Credit

You can earn up to 2% extra credit added to your final grade by working with a writing tutor at the Academic Success Center. Each visit counts as a 1% boost (up to 2 visits). You are welcome and encouraged to work with a tutor as often as you want, but only the first two visits can count as extra credit.

When Will Extra Credit Show Up?

Extra credit will not automatically appear in your Canvas grade. I will manually add it to your final grade in Self-Service at the end of the semester.

What You Can Work On

You can work with a tutor on any assignment and at any stage of the writing process. For example:

  • Brainstorming ideas or coming up with a plan for a paper, assignment, or project
  • Paraphrasing and/or quoting your scholarly research
  • A specific writing issue in one of your assignments
  • APA citations and formatting
  • Revising a paper or assignment

How It Works

1. Make an Appointment

Open Canvas and click the Penji link (the light blue penguin icon) to schedule an appointment with a writing tutor at the Academic Success Center.

2. Come Prepared

Bring your assignment, any drafts or notes you have so far, and anything else you might need for the session.

3. Take Notes During Your Visit

During your session, take notes on the feedback you receive. You can write directly on your draft or on a separate piece of paper. These notes are part of what you will submit for extra credit.

4. Get Your Confirmation Email

At the end of your visit, fill out the online form to complete your session. You will receive a confirmation email from Penji with the consultation notes from your visit.

Make Sure You Get the Right Email

The email you need is the one with the consultation notes from your visit — not just the appointment confirmation. If you don't receive this email, reach back out to the Academic Success Center.

What to Submit

Submit both of the following to the Extra Credit assignment in Canvas (found at the top of the Modules page). There are two separate assignments — one for each visit.

  1. Your notes or feedback from the session — This could be the marked-up draft you worked on, the notes you took on a separate piece of paper, or the plan you developed during the session. Take a photo or scan if handwritten.
  2. A PDF of the confirmation email — The email from Penji that shows the consultation notes from your visit (not just the appointment confirmation). See How to Save an Email as a PDF for instructions.

Important Notes

  • If you used a tutoring visit as a substitute for peer review (because you missed peer review day), that visit cannot also count as extra credit.
  • The confirmation email must show the consultation notes and must be for an assignment in this class.
  • All extra credit must be submitted before the final class of the semester.

If you need help with any of the above, don't hesitate to ask!
