Weekly Writing 8

Project 2 Brainstorming: Choosing Your Direction

Requirements

Genre: Informal brainstorming/reflection (bulleted lists, freewrites, or a mix are all fine)
Assignment AI Policy: Do not use GenAI for this assignment.

At a Glance

  • Choose your direction: Golden Age (utopian extreme) or System Failure (dystopian extreme)
  • Identify which Project 1 sources could become your real artifacts and start thinking about how they set up the story your exhibit will tell
  • Explain your reasoning

Overview

Before we start building Project 2, you need to make some decisions about the direction your exhibit will take. This assignment is about thinking through those choices — you are not locked into anything yet, but you should leave this assignment with a clearer sense of where you're headed.

Instructions

Respond to both parts below. Bulleted lists, freewrites, or a mix are all fine — whatever helps you think.

Part 1: Pick Your Direction

Are you going Golden Age or System Failure? Explain why. Think about what your Project 1 research suggested — which direction feels like a more interesting (or more fun) extreme to push toward? What about your research made you lean this way?

Part 2: Your Real Artifacts

Your exhibit starts with the present — real sources from your Project 1 research that show where things stand right now. These become the "history" section of your museum exhibit.

Look back at your Project 1 sources and identify at least 3 that could work as real artifacts. For each one:

  • What is it? (a Reddit thread, a news article, a quote from a scholarly source, etc.)
  • What makes it a good museum piece? Think about what would catch a visitor's eye or set the stage for the story your exhibit will tell. A striking quote? A surprising statistic? A heated exchange? A key finding that hints at what's coming?
  • How does it connect to your chosen direction? Does it show early warning signs (for System Failure)? Early promise (for Golden Age)? A tension or debate that your exhibit will take to an extreme?

You don't need to finalize your artifact list yet. The goal is to start seeing your research as exhibit material and to think about which pieces best set up the story you want to tell.

Grading

✓+ (2/2)
Both parts are completed thoughtfully. You've chosen a direction with reasoning connected to your research, and you've identified at least 3 potential real artifacts with clear explanations of why they'd work in your exhibit.
✓− (1/2)
One of the above parts is missing or only partially completed.
NC (0/2)
(no credit)
The assignment is not submitted, is very incomplete, or does not engage with the Project 2 concept.
